# Iraqi Dialect Guide for TSH Clients Console

## Overview
The Arabic interface uses **Iraqi dialect** (not Syrian, UAE, or Kuwaiti dialects) to provide a localized experience for Iraqi customers.

## Addressing Conventions

### Arabic (استاذ)
- Always address visitors and customers as **"استاذ"** (ustadh - Mr./Sir)
- Example: "هلا استاذ" (Hello, Sir)
- Example: "شكراً استاذ" (Thank you, Sir)

### English (Mr./Sir)
- Use **"Mr."** or **"Sir"** when addressing customers
- Example: "Welcome, Mr. {name}"
- Example: "Hello, Sir!"

## Key Iraqi Dialect Characteristics

### Common Iraqi Words & Phrases

| Iraqi Dialect | MSA (Standard) | English |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| شلون | كيف | How |
| هسة | الآن | Now |
| احنا | نحن | We |
| وايد | كثير | Very/A lot |
| هلا/هلا وغلا | مرحباً | Hello/Welcome |
| حق/حقك | لـ/الخاص بك | For/Your |
| عدنا/عندنا | لدينا | With us/We have |
| شيل همك/لا تشيل هم | لا تقلق | Don't worry |
| تگدر/تكدر | تستطيع | You can |
| لو سمحت | من فضلك | Please |
| بينا | بنا | With us |
| عالمتجر | على المتجر | On the store |
| مو | ليس/لا | Not |
| ما عندك | ليس لديك | You don't have |

### Verb Conjugations

Iraqi dialect often uses:
- **راح** (will) instead of **سوف/سـ**
  - Example: "راح نعالجه" (We will process it)
- **گدر/كدر** (can) with "گ" sound
  - Example: "شلون أگدر أساعدك" (How can I help you)

### Sentence Structure

Iraqi dialect is more casual and direct:
- **Formal MSA**: "يرجى الانتظار لحظة والمحاولة مرة أخرى"
- **Iraqi**: "لو سمحت استنى شوية وحاول مرة ثانية"

## When to Use Formal vs. Iraqi Dialect

### Use Iraqi Dialect (Informal/Conversational):
✅ Welcome messages
✅ AI assistant conversations
✅ Help text and tooltips
✅ Product descriptions (casual)
✅ Cart and shopping messages
✅ Error messages (friendly tone)
✅ Success notifications

### Use Formal Arabic (Legal/Business):
✅ Invoices and financial documents
✅ Terms and conditions
✅ Legal disclaimers
✅ Official business correspondence
✅ Formal contracts
✅ Account statements

## Translation Checklist

When adding new translations:

1. **Address the user**
   - [ ] Include "استاذ" in Arabic greetings
   - [ ] Include "Mr." or "Sir" in English equivalents

2. **Use Iraqi vocabulary**
   - [ ] Replace MSA words with Iraqi equivalents (see table above)
   - [ ] Use "راح" for future tense
   - [ ] Use "مو" for negation

3. **Keep it conversational**
   - [ ] Shorter, more direct sentences
   - [ ] Natural Iraqi phrasing
   - [ ] Avoid overly formal constructions

4. **Maintain respect**
   - [ ] Always use "استاذ" for addressing
   - [ ] Use "لو سمحت" (please) when requesting
   - [ ] Keep professional tone in business contexts
